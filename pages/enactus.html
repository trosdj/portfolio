Presentation Design

Enactus is best described by their own vision statement, "Young leaders everywhere use innovation and business skills to ensure that all people thrive in a sustainable world." By far my most influential position was as the Director of Marketing and Media for Robert Wesleyan's chapter, as well as my role as one of three designers of the annual report presentation.

My role was to do the graphic and animation work, as the others focused on the photo and video elements. This presentation is given at the national convention, and is used as both a report of the chapter's work for the year, as well as a competition being judged by representatives of Fortune 500 companies.

To give a bit of context, the work the Roberts Wesleyan chapter does is routed in education, specifically trying to combat the incredibly low graduation rate of Rochester, NY. We ran programs targeting kindergartners, fifth grade girls, and 8th - 9th grade students at risk of dropping out. The presentation was broken into sections dedicated to each program, indicated by a unique color scheme.

The presentation started by highlighting the program, Escal8, which puts a student mentor with a group of five kindergarten students to lead them through lessons in topics like sharing, organization, and other skills that may be missed at home. Interventions like these are proven to be the bedrock for a student making it all the way to graduation.

One animation, represented as three separate slides here, was to represent that out of the 500 CEOs of Fortune 500 companies only 21 were women (at the time of the presentation). When not looking deliberately for them, the purple dots are barely visible. However, when the dark dots shine a light on the disparity it is clear just how out numbered they are.
