Roberts Wesleyan
Student Association

Social Media | Digital Marketing

The Roberts Wesleyan College Student Association is in charge of all student-led issues on campus. As the in-house graphic designer, every student organization had access to request design work from me, usually to promote their on campus event.

Each of these requests would usually have a one week, or less, turnaround and the deliverable would be: a social graphic, a poster, and a digital ad for the screens across campus.

Due to the short turnaround, the design process had to be truncated quite a bit, leading me to follow a semi-strict schedule.

Usually, the day after getting the request, I would start by iterating over a few sketches. My personal philosophy is to do as many different iterations as I can think of, as the more you put on paper, the better the end result would be.

The process was always centered around the poster, as this was the deliverable most important to the client.

Over the next few days, I would create some basic mock-ups of the top three options. After they choose a direction to go, I would make edits at their request as well as change colors, fix type, or any other adjustments that will punch up the final design.

By the final day, a final design was sent to the client. After getting the approval, the final poster design was then translated into the other deliverable. Once everything was completed the deliverables were sent to the print shop and uploaded to the campus' digital screens.
